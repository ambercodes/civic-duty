# Administrative And Governance Operations

Civic Duty separates civic methodology from operational stewardship. Administrators can operate the platform, but they do not get hidden authority to alter civic outcomes or ideologically reject qualified reviews.

## Roles

### Public Participant

A user who satisfies Level 1 eligibility requirements and may read dossiers, confirm review, signal concerns, ratify, and respond to provisions.

### Verified Concern Creator

A Level 2 or higher user who may submit foundational concerns or pre-dossiers into the concern pipeline.

### Maintainer / Steward

A trusted project operator who may manage repository hygiene, public documentation, issue triage, evidence organization, release preparation, methodology documentation, and public changelogs.

### Administrator

An operational role with access to deployment, database operations, admin routes, user support, security review, archive operations, and public record publication workflows.

## Governance Rules

- threshold-qualified dossiers advance automatically
- admins do not ideologically reject qualified dossiers
- admins may manage repo, deployment, access, archives, and operations
- published CRRs are not silently changed
- changes appear in public changelogs
- forks are independent unless adopted into the main repo

## Concern And Dossier Stewardship

Maintainers and administrators may:

- group similar concerns
- identify exact duplicates
- redirect duplicates to existing or archived concerns
- preserve variants that differ meaningfully
- archive stale, superseded, or completed concerns with public reason
- reactivate archived concerns when reintroduced
- prepare threshold-qualified concerns for official dossier publication

They may not:

- rewrite an immutable pre-dossier submission
- suppress a qualified concern because of viewpoint disagreement
- silently merge materially different variants
- alter signal counts without public audit trail

## CRR Stewardship

Published Civic Ratification Records are public civic artifacts.

Allowed after publication:

- append public changelog entry
- publish correction record
- publish superseding record
- add archival continuity notes
- update external evidence availability notes

Not allowed after publication:

- silently edit ratification totals
- silently change provision results
- remove boundary statements
- rewrite methodology history
- hide valid participation

## Forks

Civic Duty is open source. Forks may adapt the framework for different civic, organizational, or methodological uses.

Forks are independent unless formally adopted into the main repository. A fork's records, methods, thresholds, and governance decisions do not become official Civic Duty records merely because they use the codebase.

## Changelogs And Auditability

Public changelogs should be used for:

- published CRR corrections
- methodology version changes
- threshold rule changes
- population baseline updates
- dossier publication updates
- archive/reactivation events
- significant deployment or data model changes

Administrative audit logs should be used for:

- role changes
- admin route writes
- security flags
- archive operations
- CRR publication operations
- manual threshold evaluation actions

## Operational Neutrality

Civic Duty measures and records structured participation. It does not persuade, campaign, enforce, adjudicate, or replace governmental process.

Operational decisions should protect scope, methodology, privacy, system availability, and public auditability without becoming ideological gatekeeping.
